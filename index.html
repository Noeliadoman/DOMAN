About Me

Hi, I'm Noelia Doman - a Computer Science student at Grand Valley State University with a deep passion for software development and problem-solving. Before returning to school, I spent nearly a decade working in the veterinary field, where I learned the importance of precision, empathy, and teamwork. During that time, I realized how powerful technology can be in solving real-world challenges, especially in healthcare. That discovery inspired me to pursue software engineering - a field where I can combine my love for helping others with my drive to create meaningful, practical solutions.

Today, I'm focused on building software that makes life easier and more efficient. Whether it is through medical applications, veterinary tools, or innovative everday technologies. My goal is to create intuitive, user-cenetered solutions that have a real impact on people's lives. I’m excited to contribute to projects that make a meaningful difference in the world.

When I'm not coding or studying, you’ll usually find me spending time with my family. My husband is a firefighter for the Grand Rapids Fire Department, we have a 3-year-old daughter and a new baby boy born in September 2025. We love boating, gardening, reading and spending time outdoors with our many animal - dogs, a cat, cows, chickens, ducks, pigs, goats, geese and even a rabbit!
